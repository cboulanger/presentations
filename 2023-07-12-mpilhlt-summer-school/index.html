Let's ask ChatGPT: <!-- .element: class="fragment" data-fragment-index="1" --> > A knowledge graph is a **structured representation of information** that captures **relationships between entities**. It is designed to organize and connect various types of knowledge, such as facts, concepts, and entities, in a **graph-like structure**. Knowledge graphs are used to model knowledge in a way that is **machine-readable** and can be easily processed and analyzed. <!-- .element: class="fragment" data-fragment-index="2" --> --- ## In particular: KG of an academic discipline captures the graph-like structure of <img style="width:30vw; float:right; margin-left: 20px;" src="./uploads/f898f341-d3da-4a96-8502-83bf27b48ddc.png" /> - academic knowledge production: works citing other works <!-- .element: class="fragment" data-fragment-index="1" --> - the social network of scholars, institutions, conferences ... <!-- .element: class="fragment" data-fragment-index="2" --> --- <!-- .slide: data-background="./uploads/2d1712fe-8c5e-4266-88d0-59cd183f4ca3.png" --> ## Data model complexity vs. data acquisition cost <img style="width:25vw; float:right; margin-left: 20px;" src="./uploads/46192067-d9bf-4325-aca5-8a4f47482869.jpg" /> Tradeoff between "richness" and data availability & usability of the KG: - must provide enough **discrete entities** (example: author node vs author metadata on publications) <!-- .element: class="fragment" data-fragment-index="1" --> - data must be **available/generatable** (example: publications vs. academic conferences) <!-- .element: class="fragment" data-fragment-index="2" --> - costs for **post-processing** in terms of time & people resources (example: OCR cleaning and correction) <!-- .element: class="fragment" data-fragment-index="3" --> --- ## Data model: Design ![](./uploads/942e44f6-abb2-419a-a288-9c00fe24db9e.png) Advantages: suffiently generic, OpenAlex.org as Open Access data provider with matching data model --- <!-- .slide: data-background="./uploads/2d1712fe-8c5e-4266-88d0-59cd183f4ca3.png" --> ## Gaps in the data for intellectual history ![](./uploads/54b82133-d42b-4944-bca6-a1acf478a918.png) --- <!-- .slide: data-background="./uploads/2d1712fe-8c5e-4266-88d0-59cd183f4ca3.png" --> ## Example workflow for generating a citation graph [![](./uploads/107d92e4-9851-487d-88bc-11dbb89c1a54.png =300x)](https://cboulanger.github.io/anystyle-workflow/workflow.drawio.sozi.html) ---- <!-- .slide: data-background="./uploads/2d1712fe-8c5e-4266-88d0-59cd183f4ca3.png" --> ## Training citation extraction models ![](./uploads/7f3937c3-fcec-4db9-b998-21434384628d.png) https://github.com/cboulanger/citext ---- <!-- .slide: data-background="./uploads/2d1712fe-8c5e-4266-88d0-59cd183f4ca3.png" --> ## Problems -
